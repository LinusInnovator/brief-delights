Gradient-based Planning for World Models at Longer Horizons (14 minute read)

GRASP is a new gradient-based planner for learned world models that enables long-horizon planning by lifting the trajectory into virtual states and adding stochasticity for exploration.

Why this matters: This allows researchers to leverage powerful world models for control, planning, and decision-making in complex, high-dimensional environments.

Pando: A Controlled Benchmark for Interpretability Methods (5 minute read)

Pando is a new interpretability benchmark with 720+ fine-tuned LLMs carrying known decision rules and varying rationale faithfulness. Gradient-based methods outperform blackbox baselines on models with no or unfaithful explanations.

Why this matters: This benchmark can guide the development of more robust and reliable interpretability techniques for safety-critical AI systems.
