Why this matters: This enables researchers and practitioners to build more powerful, scalable, and reliable reasoning models for complex real-world tasks.

Gradient-based Planning for World Models at Longer Horizons (14 minute read)

GRASP is a new gradient-based planner for learned dynamics models that enables long-horizon planning by lifting the trajectory into virtual states and adding stochasticity for exploration.

Why this matters: This allows researchers to leverage powerful world models for high-impact applications like robotic control, decision-making, and open-ended exploration.

Defending against Prompt Injection with Structured Queries (StruQ) and Preference Optimization (SecAlign) (4 minute read)

Researchers propose StruQ and SecAlign to defend against prompt injection attacks on LLMs, reducing attack success rates to near 0% without additional cost.

Why this matters: Enables secure deployment of LLMs in high-stakes applications by addressing a top vulnerability, unlocking new use cases.

What exactly does word2vec learn? (6 minute read)

The paper provides a quantitative theory for how word2vec learns, showing it reduces to unweighted least-squares matrix factorization with the final embeddings given by PCA.

Why this matters: This sheds light on the linear structure of word embeddings, enabling better understanding and control of language model representations.
