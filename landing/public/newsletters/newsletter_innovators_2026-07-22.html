AI & Innovation

Gradient-based Planning for World Models at Longer Horizons (14 minute read)

GRASP is a new gradient-based planner for learned dynamics models that enables robust long-horizon planning by leveraging virtual states and stochastic exploration.

Why this matters: This allows AI researchers to push the boundaries of what's possible with learned world models for planning, control, and decision-making.

The Berkeley Artificial Intelligence Research Blog

RL without TD learning (9 minute read)

A new RL algorithm based on divide-and-conquer, not temporal difference learning, scales well to long-horizon tasks without the limitations of traditional RL methods.

Why this matters: This opens new possibilities for applying RL to domains with expensive data collection, challenging the assumptions of existing RL algorithms.

The Berkeley Artificial Intelligence Research Blog

Whole-Body Conditioned Egocentric Video Prediction (7 minute read)

A model that predicts egocentric video from human actions, enabling simulation of counterfactuals and long-term video generation for embodied AI agents.

Why this matters: This opens new possibilities for training embodied AI agents that can plan and reason about their actions in the real world.

The Berkeley Artificial Intelligence Research Blog

Adaptive Parallel Reasoning: The Next Paradigm in Efficient Inference Scaling (15 minute read)

Adaptive Parallel Reasoning enables models to dynamically decide how to decompose and parallelize reasoning tasks, improving efficiency and scaling beyond sequential constraints.

Why this matters: This breakthrough allows researchers to tackle complex reasoning problems that were previously intractable, opening new frontiers for AI systems.

The Berkeley Artificial Intelligence Research Blog

What exactly does word2vec learn? (6 minute read)

The paper provides a theory explaining how the word2vec algorithm learns representations, showing it reduces to unweighted least-squares matrix factorization with a closed-form solution.

Why this matters: This sheds light on representation learning in minimal language models, informing research on more sophisticated architectures like large language models.

The Berkeley Artificial Intelligence Research Blog

Defending against Prompt Injection with Structured Queries (StruQ) and Preference Optimization (SecAlign) (4 minute read)

Researchers propose StruQ and SecAlign to defend LLMs against prompt injection attacks, a top threat to LLM-integrated applications, by separating prompt and data, and training LLMs to ignore injected instructions.

Why this matters: This enables secure deployment of LLMs in high-stakes applications, removing a critical vulnerability and unlocking new use cases.

The Berkeley Artificial Intelligence Research Blog

Enhancing Rubric-based RL via Self-Distillation (2 minute read)

Researchers propose Criterion-Distilled Policy Optimization (CriPO) to address limitations of rubric-based reinforcement learning, achieving stronger performance with fewer optimization steps.

Why this matters: This enables more efficient and robust RL training for open-ended tasks, with implications for language model fine-tuning and beyond.
