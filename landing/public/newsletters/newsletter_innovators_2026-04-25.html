What exactly does word2vec learn? (6 minute read)

Researchers provide a quantitative theory explaining how word2vec learns word embeddings, showing it reduces to unweighted least-squares matrix factorization with PCA-derived representations.

Why this matters: This sheds light on the linear structure of word embeddings, enabling better understanding and control of language model representations.

Gradient-based Planning for World Models at Longer Horizons (14 minute read)

GRASP is a new gradient-based planner for learned dynamics models that enables long-horizon planning by lifting trajectories, adding stochasticity, and reshaping gradients.

Why this matters: This allows researchers and practitioners to leverage powerful world models for control, planning, and decision-making at longer time horizons.

Defending against Prompt Injection with Structured Queries (StruQ) and Preference Optimization (SecAlign) (4 minute read)

Researchers propose two defenses, StruQ and SecAlign, to mitigate the critical threat of prompt injection attacks against large language models (LLMs) in real-world applications.

Why this matters: These defenses enable secure deployment of LLMs in high-stakes applications like content moderation and decision support systems.
