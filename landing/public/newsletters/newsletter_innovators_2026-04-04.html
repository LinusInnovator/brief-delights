Scaling Up Reinforcement Learning for Traffic Smoothing: A 100-AV Highway Deployment (9 minute read)

Deployed 100 RL-controlled cars to smooth highway traffic, reducing congestion and fuel waste. Trained efficient flow-smoothing controllers in simulations before field deployment.

Why this matters: Researchers can build on this to develop scalable, decentralized traffic control solutions using RL and standard vehicle sensors.

Virtual Personas for Language Models via an Anthology of Backstories (5 minute read)

Anthology conditions language models with detailed backstories to generate consistent, representative virtual personas for user research and social sciences.

Why this matters: This allows researchers to conduct cost-effective pilot studies and validate best practices for human studies.

The Berkeley Artificial Intelligence Research Blog

Linguistic Bias in ChatGPT: Language Models Reinforce Dialect Discrimination (4 minute read)

ChatGPT exhibits pervasive biases against non-standard English varieties, including increased stereotyping, poorer comprehension, and condescending responses.

Why this matters: Researchers must address linguistic biases in AI models to ensure equitable access and avoid perpetuating real-world discrimination.

The Berkeley Artificial Intelligence Research Blog

Defending against Prompt Injection with Structured Queries (StruQ) and Preference Optimization (SecAlign) (4 minute read)

Researchers propose StruQ and SecAlign, fine-tuning defenses that reduce prompt injection attack success rates to near-zero without added cost or labor.

Why this matters: These defenses enable safer deployment of LLM-powered applications, reducing risks of malicious manipulation of model outputs.

The Berkeley Artificial Intelligence Research Blog

Repurposing Protein Folding Models for Generation with Latent Diffusion (6 minute read)

PLAID is a multimodal generative model that can simultaneously generate protein sequence and 3D structure, by learning the latent space of protein folding models.

Why this matters: This opens new possibilities for AI-driven protein design and drug discovery, by allowing researchers to specify complex constraints and generate 'useful' proteins.

The Berkeley Artificial Intelligence Research Blog

Whole-Body Conditioned Egocentric Video Prediction (7 minute read)

A model that predicts egocentric video from human actions, enabling simulation of counterfactuals and long-term video generation for embodied agents.

Why this matters: Allows AI systems to better understand and predict the consequences of their actions in the real world.

The Berkeley Artificial Intelligence Research Blog

What exactly does word2vec learn? (6 minute read)

Researchers provide a quantitative theory for how word2vec learns, showing it reduces to unweighted least-squares matrix factorization with embeddings expanding into linear subspaces.

Why this matters: This reveals fundamental principles behind representation learning in minimal language models, informing development of more sophisticated architectures.

The Berkeley Artificial Intelligence Research Blog

RL without TD learning (9 minute read)

A new RL algorithm based on divide-and-conquer, not TD learning, scales well to long-horizon tasks, addressing key limitations of traditional RL methods.

Why this matters: This opens new possibilities for applying RL to high-stakes, long-term decision-making domains like robotics, healthcare, and dialogue systems.

The Berkeley Artificial Intelligence Research Blog
