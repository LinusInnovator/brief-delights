Scaling Up Reinforcement Learning for Traffic Smoothing: A 100-AV Highway Deployment (9 minute read)

Researchers deployed 100 self-driving cars on a highway to smooth traffic flow and reduce fuel consumption using reinforcement learning controllers.

Why this matters: This opens a scalable path to addressing ubiquitous traffic congestion without costly infrastructure, challenging assumptions about the role of AVs.

Virtual Personas for Language Models via an Anthology of Backstories (5 minute read)

A method to steer language models to generate consistent, diverse virtual personas by conditioning on richly detailed backstories, enabling more realistic user research and social science studies.

Why this matters: Enables researchers to conduct cost-effective pilot studies and best practices in human studies by using virtual personas grounded in naturalistic backstories.

Linguistic Bias in ChatGPT: Language Models Reinforce Dialect Discrimination (4 minute read)

ChatGPT exhibits pervasive biases against non-standard English varieties, with poorer comprehension, increased stereotyping, and condescending responses compared to standard varieties.

Why this matters: Researchers and AI practitioners must address linguistic biases to ensure language models are equitable and inclusive for all users.

Defending against Prompt Injection with Structured Queries (StruQ) and Preference Optimization (SecAlign) (4 minute read)

Researchers propose StruQ and SecAlign, two fine-tuning defenses against prompt injection attacks that preserve utility and reduce attack success rates to near 0%.

Why this matters: These defenses enable researchers and practitioners to build more secure LLM-powered systems, a critical need as LLMs become ubiquitous.

The Berkeley Artificial Intelligence Research Blog

RL without TD learning (9 minute read)

A new reinforcement learning algorithm based on divide-and-conquer, not temporal difference (TD) learning, scales well to long-horizon tasks.

Why this matters: This opens new possibilities for applying RL to complex, long-horizon problems where data collection is expensive.

The Berkeley Artificial Intelligence Research Blog

Repurposing Protein Folding Models for Generation with Latent Diffusion (6 minute read)

PLAID is a multimodal generative model that can simultaneously generate protein sequences and 3D structures, by learning the latent space of protein folding models.

Why this matters: This opens new possibilities for AI-driven protein design and drug discovery, by allowing researchers to specify desired protein properties and generate matching sequences and structures.

The Berkeley Artificial Intelligence Research Blog

What exactly does word2vec learn? (6 minute read)

The paper provides a quantitative theory explaining how word2vec learns representations, showing it reduces to unweighted least-squares matrix factorization with PCA-like dynamics.

Why this matters: This sheds light on the linear structure in language model representations, enabling better understanding and more principled model development.

The Berkeley Artificial Intelligence Research Blog

Whole-Body Conditioned Egocentric Video Prediction (7 minute read)

A model called PEVA can predict egocentric video from human actions, enabling simulation of counterfactuals and long-term video generation for embodied AI agents.

Why this matters: This opens new possibilities for AI systems to learn and plan in physically grounded, egocentric environments.

The Berkeley Artificial Intelligence Research Blog
